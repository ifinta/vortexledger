Important information about the "Exchange Bot" on the Byteball Ledger:

If you type 'book' then you don't see the right sizes (Standard Sizes)...
They are mostly cumulative values - they shows a cumulative value for more similar smart contracts, oft with
different amounts and some prices. It is a luck, if they shows only one smart contract.

At this time the order book shows as below:


But it exists 16 orders!

Standard Sizes are allways power of 2.
Charm has 6 decimals. How should we calculate then the Standard Sizes?
See the queue below (only some first values, to show the rules):

0.000001
0.000002
0.000004
0.000008
0.000016
...
0.001024
...
1.048576
...

I used OpenOffice Calc to calculate this queue.

Please use the Standard Sizes - as we do (see our commands as example to sell or to buy):

our command to sell/buy Charm's:

sell 8589.934592 at 0.000001
buy 8589.934592 at 0.0000007

you should type:

buy 8589.934592 at 0.000001
sell 8589.934592 at 0.0000007

If you don't use the Standard Sizes - your order can remain open - without to fullfill your will!!!
Or the order succeeds only partially, because your order will be truncated and break into parts with standard sizes - and you pay more fee's!
